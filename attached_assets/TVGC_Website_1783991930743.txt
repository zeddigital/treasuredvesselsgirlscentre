WHO WE ARE
Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre is a fully registered non-governmental (charitable) community based organization (CBO) in Eastern Uganda, Jinja District, founded in 2018 by Madam Racheal Muggaga Achen. This CBO reaches out to vulnerable women and girls who are left without adequate care and support due to: the deaths of HIV/AIDS affected persons; remarriage of deserted, widowed, or divorced women; absence of love
and security in the families; family disputes; and/or unwanted pregnancy of trafficked or sexually exploited girls.


Through different activities focused on empowering women and girls, Treasured Vessels reaches out in schools, institutions, and youth programs with training in practical skills. In these activities, we work at grass root level with communities to reach and support victims of GBV (Gender Based Violence), to rehabilitate and to address community health challenges by creating awareness.

Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre is a women-led, non-governmental organization dedicated to empowering girls and young women in Soroti District. We focus on education, skills training, advocacy, and social support to help girls overcome challenges such as teenage pregnancies, early marriages, and gender-based violence. What are the main programs of Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre? Our key programs include:Education Support: Scholarships, school re-entry programs, and mentorship.Economic Empowerment: Vocational skills training and entrepreneurship support.Girls’ Rights Advocacy: Raising awareness on gender equality and child protection.Health & Reproductive Rights: Sensitization on sexual health, family planning, and menstrual hygiene.Social Support & Rehabilitation: Counseling, shelter, and support for vulnerable girls. Who can benefit from Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre programs? Our programs are designed for:Vulnerable girls and young women facing poverty, abuse, or school dropout.Teenage mothers who need skills training and support.Victims of gender-based violence and early marriages.Young women seeking economic empowerment and self-reliance. How does Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre help girls who have dropped out of school? We provide alternative education and skills training to ensure that girls who have dropped out due to pregnancy, forced marriage, or financial struggles can rebuild their future. Our support includes vocational training, counseling, and school reintegration programs. How does Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre support teenage mothers? We provide counseling, life skills training, parenting education, and economic empowerment opportunities to help teenage mothers become self-reliant and care for their children. What role do parents and the community play in the Centre’s work? We actively engage parents, local leaders, and community members to:Raise awareness about girls’ rights and education.Prevent early marriages and gender-based violence.Provide support and mentorship to vulnerable girls. How can I support or donate to Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre? You can support us by:Donating funds or resources (school materials, sanitary pads, vocational tools, etc.).Sponsoring a girl’s education or skills training.Volunteering your expertise in training, mentoring, or advocacy. Does Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre provide shelter for at-risk girls? Yes, we offer temporary shelter and emergency support for girls facing abuse, abandonment, or teenage pregnancy, while working to reintegrate them into a safe environment. How can a girl or young woman join the Centre’s programs? Girls and young women in need can reach out to us directly, be referred by community leaders, or apply through our local office. We assess each case and offer the most suitable support. How can organizations or partners collaborate with Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre? We welcome partnerships with NGOs, government agencies, community groups, and donors to expand our impact.

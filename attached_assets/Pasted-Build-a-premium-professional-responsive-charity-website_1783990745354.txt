Build a premium, professional, responsive charity website for Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre, a women-led, registered community organisation based in Eastern Uganda and founded in 2018.

The organisation supports vulnerable girls, teenage mothers and women affected by poverty, interrupted education, teenage pregnancy, early marriage, gender-based violence, exploitation, abandonment and family instability.

Its work includes girls’ education and sponsorship, school reintegration, vocational skills training, teenage-mother support, menstrual health education, school outreach, counselling and referrals, gender-rights advocacy, community relief and practical economic empowerment.

PRIMARY WEBSITE GOALS

1. Clearly explain who Treasured Vessels Girls’ Centre is and what it does.
2. Establish credibility, professionalism, transparency and emotional trust.
3. Encourage one-time and monthly donations.
4. Promote girl sponsorship, partnerships, volunteering and in-kind donations.
5. Showcase authentic program images and measurable impact.
6. Publish stories, project updates and organisational reports.
7. Provide a safe pathway for girls, families and community leaders to request help or make referrals.

BRAND POSITIONING

The website must feel hopeful, dignified, trustworthy, compassionate, locally grounded and professionally managed.

Do not use a generic charity-template appearance. Do not present girls and women as helpless. Show them as capable people receiving practical support, gaining skills, rebuilding confidence and creating stronger futures.

The visual tone should combine warmth, human connection and community authenticity with the polish expected from an internationally credible non-profit organisation.

TYPOGRAPHY

Use DM Serif Display for headings and Inter for body text, navigation, forms and buttons.

The heading style should feel warm, editorial and authoritative. Body text must be highly readable and accessible.

DESIGN STYLE

Use a clean editorial layout with generous whitespace, large authentic photography, soft rounded corners and restrained shadows.

Maximum content width: approximately 1240px.
Use large section spacing.
Cards should have 18–24px rounded corners.
Images should have 20–28px rounded corners.
Buttons should have 8–12px rounded corners.
Do not make every component pill-shaped.

Use subtle rounded line icons for education, vocational skills, teenage-mother support, health, protection, partnerships and community outreach.

Use subtle animations only: gentle fade-ins, number counters, image reveals and refined hover states. Do not use distracting sliders, flying text or excessive parallax.

PHOTOGRAPHY

Use the supplied authentic Treasured Vessels photographs throughout the website.

Prioritise images showing active participation, training, mentoring, finished products, group learning, celebration, community connection and confidence.

Use warm natural colour correction, consistent crops and gentle rounded corners.

Do not overuse repeated group photographs.
Do not use pity-based imagery.
Do not use generic stock photos where authentic project photos are available.
Do not publish sensitive identifying information about vulnerable children or survivors.

ACCESSIBILITY AND PERFORMANCE

Design mobile-first and ensure the site works well on slower internet connections.

Compress and lazy-load images.
Use clear colour contrast.
Use visible keyboard focus states.
Use descriptive alt text.
Use accessible form labels.
Use large tap targets.
Avoid autoplay video.
Avoid heavy animations.

TRUST AND SAFEGUARDING

Include visible links to:
Registration and governance information
Safeguarding policy
Privacy policy
Complaints and feedback
Donation management
Annual or project reports

The website must protect the identity and dignity of vulnerable girls, teenage mothers and survivors of violence.

Do not invent statistics, partner logos, testimonials, registration numbers, donation amounts or program outcomes. Use clearly labelled placeholders where confirmed information has not been supplied.

The final website should feel polished enough to present to international donors, NGOs, foundations, churches, businesses and government partners while remaining warm, accessible and authentically connected to the communities Treasured Vessels serves.
